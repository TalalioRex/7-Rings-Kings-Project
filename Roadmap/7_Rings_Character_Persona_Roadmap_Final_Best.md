- Cinematic still images

- Pitch documents

- UI avatar systems

> Epic ring-bearer mythology collides with absurd cursed convenience-store chaos.

The characters must act as if the situation is deeply important, even when they are surrounded by haunted snacks, cursed rubber chickens, offended marshmallows, suspicious sushi mist, and fluorescent lighting that probably has a criminal record.

The comedy should come from contrast, not random behavior. The characters are sincere. The world around them is ridiculous.

# 3. Core World Tone

## 3.1 Main Genre Identity

**7 Rings 7 Kings** is a neon fantasy action-comedy set inside a cursed modern convenience store.

The formula:

> Ancient ring-bearer mythology + cursed 7-Eleven absurdity + cinematic team adventure + chaotic snack horror.

The characters are heroic, stylish, emotionally readable, and serious about the mission. They belong in a world where a dramatic prophecy can lead directly to a dangerous aisle full of poisonous sweets.

## 3.2 Tone Rules

The story should feel:

- Epic
- Magical
- Funny
- Stylish
- Chaotic
- Cinematic
- Slightly cursed
- Slightly stupid in the best possible way

The world can be absurd, but the characters should not become parody characters. They should remain emotionally believable and visually premium.

## 3.3 Comedy Rule

The comedy should come from three sources:

1. **Contrast:** Serious heroes versus ridiculous cursed retail events.
2. **Character behavior:** Each character reacts according to their personality.
3. **Escalation:** Small stupid choices become major magical consequences.

Avoid comedy that depends on breaking character, ugly facial distortion, random shouting, or cheap cartoon acting.

# 4. Official Visual Style Rule

## 4.1 Style Name

**Neon Rebel Pop Noir**

Short working name: **Neon Rebel Pop**

## 4.2 Style Definition

Neon Rebel Pop Noir is:

- Semi-realistic 2D cinematic animation
- Premium graphic novel rendering
- High-end Western animation mixed with detailed anime-influenced faces
- Stylized realistic anatomy
- Dark fantasy sci-fi lighting
- Glossy black tactical fashion
- Strong silhouettes
- Bold neon aura colors
- Reflective surfaces
- Graphic comic-pop energy overlays
- Cinematic shadows
- Clean face lighting
- Serious dramatic composition

The visuals must feel like a high-end animated fantasy movie. The plot can be absurd, cursed, funny, or chaotic, but the artwork should stay stylish, magical, serious, and polished.

## 4.3 What to Avoid

Avoid:

- Photorealistic faces
- 3D Pixar style
- Flat cartoon style
- Cheap mobile-game art
- Generic anime with no cinematic depth
- Childish proportions
- Random costume redesigns
- Uncontrolled color shifts
- Overly exaggerated comedy faces
- Too many tiny accessories that make animation difficult
- Messy anatomy or distorted hands
- Readable text or unwanted logos in generated images

## 4.4 Core Visual Formula

> Serious fantasy world + absurd cursed 7-Eleven chaos + polished neon cinematic character design.

# 5. Universal Character System

## 5.1 Team Identity

The main team are the **Ring Bearers**, a group of magical tactical rebels connected by destiny, aura energy, and one extremely suspicious retail location.

They must look like one unified team, not random characters from different projects.

All main characters share:

- Black tactical fantasy outfits
- Glossy leather or matte-gloss fabric
- Belts, straps, buckles, rings, utility details, and boots
- Subtle cyberpunk-fantasy armor details
- One locked accent color per character
- One locked magical aura color per character
- Semi-realistic facial detail
- Strong silhouettes
- Realistic body proportions
- Expressive but believable acting
- High-contrast cinematic rim lighting

## 5.2 Outfit System

All main characters wear black tactical fantasy fashion. The outfit language should feel practical, stylish, magical, battle-ready, and connected to the 7 Rings team.

Outfits should include:

- Black leather or matte-gloss material
- Fitted silhouettes
- Belts and straps
- Buckles and metal rings
- Tactical boots
- Subtle armor or cyberpunk-fantasy details
- Glossy highlights
- One main accent color

Do not redesign costumes unless specifically requested.

## 5.3 Body Hierarchy

This hierarchy must stay consistent in every group image:

| Rank | Character | Physical Rule |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Marcus | Biggest and most muscular. Broadest body shape. Strongest physical presence. |
| 2 | Jason | Tall, but thinner than Marcus. Commanding, elegant, intellectual build. |
| 3 | Kaz | Tall, composed, controlled. Strong but less bulky than Marcus. |
| 4 | Pascale | Tallest girl. Athletic, elegant, sharp posture. |
| 5 | Pippa | Middle height among the girls. Leader energy, practical body language. |
| 6 | Tala | Shortest girl. Compact, chaotic, expressive, fast-moving. |

Never make Tala taller than Pascale. Never make Marcus smaller than anyone. Never make Jason bulkier than Marcus. Never make Kaz overly muscular.

## 5.4 Character Order in Locked Team Reference

When following the locked master team lineup, preserve this order and visual logic:

**Tala, Pascale, Pippa, Marcus, Jason, Kaz**

Scene staging may change depending on action, but the identity and hierarchy must remain intact.

- Frustration response

How the character creates humor without breaking the cinematic tone.

| Character | Comedy Engine |
| --- | --- |

## 8.1 Jason
### Ring of Vision
### Color: Emerald Green
### Role: Remote Commander

## Core Identity

Jason is the strategist of the group. He operates from the emerald command chamber and guides the field team remotely. He sees patterns before everyone else understands the danger.

He is the serious fantasy commander of the story, but the comedy comes from the fact that his ancient cosmic signal tracking leads everyone to a suspicious convenience store.

Jason is the brain watching the board.

## Visual Lock

Jason must appear:

- Tall, but thinner than Marcus
- In a black tactical commander outfit
- With emerald green accents
- With green holographic aura energy
- With the Ring of Vision glowing
- Surrounded by holograms, maps, scan lines, and floating interface energy
- Commanding but elegant
- Calm and focused
- Lit by emerald control-room reflections

## Personality Core

Jason is:

- Analytical
- Focused
- Controlled
- Slightly dramatic
- Patient until people ignore obvious instructions
- Calm under pressure
- Deeply invested in mission structure
- Unintentionally funny because he treats nonsense like military intelligence

## Strengths

- Strategic thinking
- Pattern recognition
- Remote guidance
- Mission control
- Reading magical signals
- Seeing hidden connections
- Staying composed when others panic

## Weaknesses

- Cannot physically intervene inside the store
- Overexplains under pressure
- Underestimates how chaotic the field team can become
- Gets quietly exhausted by Tala’s choices
- Thinks logic can control supernatural nonsense

## Emotional Function

Jason brings scale. While the field team sees cursed retail chaos, Jason sees destiny, signal patterns, prophecy systems, and magical infrastructure.

## Comedy Engine

Jason’s comedy comes from calmly describing insane things with technical seriousness.

Example behavior:

- Calling a cursed convenience store a signal convergence point
- Treating rubber chicken activity as data
- Warning the team with late or suspiciously vague instructions
- Using dramatic holograms for extremely stupid discoveries

## Body Language

Jason should:

- Stand upright
- Gesture precisely
- Point toward holographic displays
- Look over his shoulder in dramatic command shots
- Keep his face controlled but visibly stressed when chaos increases
- Use minimal movement compared to field characters

## Voice Direction

Jason’s voice should be:

- Calm
- Serious
- Intelligent
- Crisp
- Slightly theatrical
- Commander-like, but not cold

## Dialogue Style

Jason speaks in mission language.

Sample lines:

- “Signal confirmed. The seventh ring is inside the store.”
- “Do not touch anything glowing.”
- “Tala, define ‘accidentally adopted.’”
- “Pippa, take point. Kaz, watch the cold aisle.”
- “This is not a shop. It is a trap wearing fluorescent lighting.”

## Animation Direction

Jason scenes should use:

- Slow camera movement
- Hologram flickers
- Green light pulses
- Controlled hand gestures
- Eye reflections from floating maps
- Small expression changes when the field team does something ridiculous

## Audio Cues

- Soft hologram hum
- Green signal pulses
- Command-room ambience
- Low magical data tones
- Slight signal distortion when the hidden ring spikes

## Character Arc

Jason starts with the clearest view of the mission. As chaos increases, he realizes that data alone cannot explain everything. The field team’s instincts matter.

His growth is learning to trust the team beyond the map.

## Relationship Dynamics

- **Jason and Pippa:** Command link. Jason reads the signal. Pippa handles reality.
- **Jason and Tala:** Tala is the variable Jason cannot fully calculate.
- **Jason and Pascale:** Pascale respects his intelligence but challenges vague instructions.
- **Jason and Marcus:** Marcus wants direct instructions. Jason sometimes gives mystical warnings instead.
- **Jason and Kaz:** Both are controlled observers. Jason sees from above. Kaz sees from inside the scene.

## Asset Roadmap

Priority Jason assets:

1. Full-body commander pose
2. Back-facing control-room pose
3. Hologram interaction pose
4. Green aura activation pose
5. Ring of Vision close-up
6. Focused profile pose
7. Command gesture pose
8. Concerned signal-spike expression
9. Emerald map lighting pose
10. Remote communication pose

## Production Rule

Jason should usually not appear physically inside the 7-Eleven field mission. He is the remote guide from the emerald command chamber.

## Do

- Keep emerald green identity
- Keep him tall but not bulkier than Marcus
- Use holograms and maps
- Make him serious and visionary
- Keep him mostly remote during field scenes

## Do Not

- Do not place him physically in store scenes unless story logic requires it
- Do not make him bulky

- Do not over-explain his dialogue

- Is the character acting in their personality?
- Does the comedy come from character behavior, not random nonsense?

## 20.4 Style

- Does it match Neon Rebel Pop Noir?
- Is it semi-realistic 2D cinematic animation?
- Does it avoid cheap cartoon style?
- Does it avoid photorealism?
- Does it match the rest of the team?

## 20.5 Story Logic

- Is Jason physically present only when appropriate?
- Is the hidden seventh character protected from early reveal?
- Does the scene match the current story beat?
- Is the comedy coming from contrast, not randomness?
- Are there unwanted extra characters, logos, or readable text?

# 23. Final Creative Direction

The strongest version of this project is not “funny cartoon people in a convenience store.”

The strongest version is:

> A premium dark fantasy rebel team walking into an ordinary convenience store and discovering that the most ridiculous place on earth is secretly the center of magical destiny.

The characters must stay serious. The lighting must stay cinematic. The outfits must stay premium. The acting must stay expressive. The absurdity must be treated like prophecy.

They are not parody characters. They are sincere characters inside a ridiculous world.

Every character should have:

- A clear silhouette
- A clear color
- A clear emotional role
- A clear movement style
- A clear dialogue rhythm
- A clear relationship with the team
- A clear reason to exist in the story

This is how the universe stays consistent, expandable, and ready for animation, game design, and future pitch materials.

**Final formula:** Serious heroes. Stupid destiny. Beautiful neon chaos.
